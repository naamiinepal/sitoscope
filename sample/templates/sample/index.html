About Project

Diarrheal diseases are one of the major causes of death and morbidity among children under the age of five in Nepal. This disease caused by the consumption of food and water contaminated with parasites namely giardia & cryptosporidium can only be prevented via detection of these parasites. Brightfield microscope mostly used in the context of Nepal for these detections has not been suitable in our geographically diverse region as it requires expensive lab setups, experts and other costly reagents. A low-cost alternative method for these resource-constrained settings is to use a smartphone microscope that is accessible, transportable and easy to use where images can be used to derive real-time automatic detection of diarrhea-causing protozoa.
